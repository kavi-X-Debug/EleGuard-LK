🐘 About EleGuard
EleGuard is a smart, software-driven mobile application designed to protect Sri Lankan farmers from wild elephant intrusions. By processing seismic signals detected from geophone sensors placed along farm boundaries, EleGuard provides real-time alerts, live heatmap visualization, and predictive insights — giving farmers the early warning they need to protect their lives and crops before an elephant reaches their land.
The name EleGuard reflects its core purpose — an intelligent guardian against elephant intrusions, built specifically for the farming communities living on the edge of Sri Lanka's wildlife borders.
---
🌿 The Problem
Human–Elephant Conflict (HEC) is one of the most serious ongoing challenges in rural Sri Lanka. Elephants routinely cross into agricultural land at night, destroying crops, damaging property, and putting farmers and their families in direct danger. Each year, dozens of people and elephants lose their lives as a result of these encounters.
Existing solutions such as electric fences and manual watchmen are costly to maintain, often fail without warning, and provide no intelligent early detection. Farmers currently have no reliable way to know an elephant is approaching until it is already inside their farm — by which point it is too late to respond safely.
There is a clear need for an affordable, intelligent, software-based system that can detect elephant movement early, alert farmers instantly, and reduce the number of dangerous encounters between humans and elephants.
---
🔬 How Detection Works
EleGuard is inspired by cutting-edge research on seismic signal-based elephant detection. When an elephant walks, its footsteps generate low-frequency seismic vibrations that travel through the ground. Geophone sensors buried along the farm boundary pick up these vibrations and measure their amplitude and frequency patterns.
The system analyzes this data to distinguish elephant movement from other ground vibrations such as vehicles or small animals, using amplitude thresholds and pattern recognition to filter out false alarms. Detections are classified into three severity levels:
🟡 LOW — Distant movement detected, elephant far from boundary
🟠 MEDIUM — Elephant approaching, increased signal strength
🔴 HIGH — Elephant very close to or at the boundary, immediate danger
Since real geophone hardware is outside the scope of this project, sensor data is simulated through the companion EleGuard Simulator app, which replicates the behavior of 14 sensors placed around a rectangular farm boundary.
> 📄 **Research Reference:** J. L. Wijayaraja et al., *"Event Detection and Classification for Long Range Sensing of Elephants Using Seismic Signals,"* IEEE Access, vol. 13, pp. 158366–158384, 2025.

🎯 Aim of the Project
The aim of EleGuard is to design and develop a smart elephant intrusion alert and prediction system that:
Provides real-time alerts to farmers the moment an elephant is detected near their farm boundary
Displays a live heatmap showing exactly which sensor zones are active, the elephant's estimated position, and its direction of movement
Delivers critical push notifications powerful enough to wake a sleeping farmer — with alarm sounds, SOS vibration patterns, and full-screen alerts
Stores a complete history of all intrusion events, allowing farmers and authorities to identify patterns and high-risk zones over time
Applies Object-Oriented Programming principles — Encapsulation, Inheritance, Abstraction, and Polymorphism — to ensure the system is modular, scalable, and maintainable
Ultimately, EleGuard aims to reduce human–elephant conflict in Sri Lanka by giving farmers the information and time they need to respond safely — protecting both human lives and the elephants themselves.
